Where's our waiter? Restaurants face labor shortage crisis

By: Lynn Ma

October 6, 2022

Restaurants across the nation are experiencing the challenge of labor shortage. They have reported a loss of workers in both the kitchen and the front of house since the COVID-19 pandemic. Many workers did not return to work, and have chosen to seek other paths. Thus, restaurants continue to struggle to find and keep employees.

Owners, workers, and customers are all experiencing the burden and consequences of the labor crisis. Lack of workers means longer waits for customers, extra work for the staff and owners and an inability to increase business revenue. Workers are leaving for more money, consistent schedules and professional development opportunities. Furthermore, childcare and mental health are cited as reasons for leaving the industry.

In order to address the understaffing problem, restaurants are resorting to desperate measures. Leland Eating and Drinking House and Amorina Cucina Rustica in Brooklyn, for example, are physically removing tables, cutting down menu options, and adapting technological automation to require less hours of labor from staff.

The issue shows no sign of slowing down. How and how much the restaurant experience will change due to this issue remain to be seen.
